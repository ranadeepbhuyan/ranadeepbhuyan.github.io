Why Pivot? The Accessibility Gap

The scientific value proposition shifts from "just another segmenter" to "democratizing advanced diagnostics." We generate expensive, invasive PET features from cheap, safe MRI.

Executive Summary

Current Issue: The original paper's focus on Tumor Segmentation is saturated. Furthermore, predicting tumor "function" from structure is controversial due to neurovascular uncoupling.

The Solution: Pivot PAGODA-Net to Cross-Modality Synthesis. Instead of drawing boundaries (segmentation), the model will generate pixel-level metabolic maps (Virtual PET) from structural inputs.

New Hypothesis: "Deep Generative Networks can capture non-linear correlations between structural atrophy (MRI) and metabolic hypometabolism (PET) to predict disease biomarkers before they become visible to the human eye."

Action Item: Retain the Dual-Stream Architecture but change the loss function from Dice (Overlap) to L1/Perceptual (Reconstruction).

The Scientific Gap

Redefining the problem statement for higher impact.

Old: Tumor Segmentation

  • Problem: "Where is the tumor?"
  • Saturation: Highly saturated (BraTS challenges).
  • Bio-Coupling: Weak. Tumors grow chaotically. Structure does not reliably predict function/eloquence due to plasticity.
  • Verdict: Hard to publish in top-tier journals without massive novelty.

New: Cross-Modality Synthesis

  • Problem: "What is the metabolic state?" (Invisible on MRI).
  • Saturation: Emerging/High-Value.
  • Bio-Coupling: Strong. In AD and TBI, structural damage (axonal shearing, atrophy) directly correlates with metabolic drop-off.
  • Verdict: "Virtual Biopsy" concept is highly publishable.

Expanded Clinical Domains

Beyond Alzheimer's: Demonstrating Robustness and Generalizability.

1. Alzheimer's Disease (AD)

Primary Focus
🧠

The Input (Source)

T1-weighted MRI. Shows cortical thickness and hippocampal volume.

The Output (Target)

FDG-PET. Shows glucose metabolism. AD brains show "hypometabolism" in the temporal lobe often before atrophy is visible.

Why here? ADNI offers thousands of paired MRI/PET scans. This is the ideal "proof of concept" dataset.

2. Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI)

Secondary / Generalization
🤕

The Input (Source)

T1/T2 MRI + DTI (Diffusion Tensor Imaging). Shows structural white matter tracts.

The Output (Target)

Tau-PET or Functional Outcome Maps. Shows chronic inflammation or specific functional deficits.

Scientific Angle: Diffuse Axonal Injury (DAI) is often "invisible" on standard MRI. PAGODA-Net can attempt to synthesize the metabolic correlates of DAI from subtle texture changes in the MRI.
Dataset: FITBIR (Federal Interagency Traumatic Brain Injury Research).

3. Motor Function Mapping

Tertiary / Clinical Utility
🖐️

Goal: Generate fMRI activation maps (Motor Cortex) from structural MRI alone.
Use Case: Pre-surgical planning for patients who cannot perform tasks (sedated/children).
Dataset: Human Connectome Project (HCP) - offers high-quality structural + task-based fMRI data.

Loss Function Overhaul

Dice Score is useless for synthesis. We must adopt:

  • L1 Loss: For pixel-level accuracy.
  • Perceptual Loss (VGG): To maintain textural sharpness.
  • SSIM: To measure structural similarity.

The "Invisible" Hypothesis

By using the ILAG (Information-Linked Attenuation Gateway), we hypothesize that subtle texture variations in the MRI (caused by micro-scale atrophy) can be amplified to predict the macro-scale metabolic drop seen in PET.
